Issue #25 Week 3 Recap • Week 4 Preview April 2026
Records Fall, Contenders Rise
Glory's Fautali leads MVP race? • Longest FG in WNFC history • Storm surge to 2-1
Week 3 of the WNFC season delivered another slate of compelling action, headlined by Maria Fautali's continued dominance and the Mississippi Panthers' road statement in Atlanta. Fautali needed just six carries to rack up 111 rushing yards and 2 touchdowns — an absurd 18.5 yards per carry — as the Kansas City Glory dispatched Oregon 23-14 at home. The Panthers continued their defensive surge with a 25-15 victory over the Atlanta Truth, dropping Atlanta to 1-2 on the season. The San Diego Rebellion kept rolling with a 24-6 win in Las Vegas to improve to 3-0 — the best record in the league, while the Golden State Storm improved to 2-1 with a 23-18 road victory over the winless Seattle Majestics. Los Angeles kept pace in the Pacific Division with a 23-0 shutout of the Tennessee Trojans, who remain scoreless through three weeks. With Week 4 featuring just four games, the spotlight falls squarely on Arlington, Texas, where the undefeated Spartans host the Chicago Winds in the week's marquee matchup.

Week 3 Recaps
Lawrenceville, GA
Mississippi Panthers25
Atlanta Truth15

The Panthers pulled off the comeback of the season, rallying from a 15-0 deficit to defeat Atlanta 25-15 on the road. Shardonay Jenkins' dominant 2.5-sack, 1 forced fumble performance helped swing momentum, while Sckiya Banister exploded for 92 rushing yards and 2 touchdowns on just 7 carries (13.1 YPC). Regena Jackson added 67 rushing yards and a score as Mississippi's offense came alive in the second half. Zoe Jackson hauled in an 83-yard catch for the Truth, but it wasn't enough as the Panthers' defense clamped down when it mattered most. Atlanta drops to 1-2 and 0-2 at home.

Long Beach, CA
Los Angeles Legends23
Tennessee Trojans0

The Legends recorded their first shutout of the season, blanking Tennessee 23-0 at home. Meagan Curtis powered the ground game with 106 rushing yards and a touchdown on 16 carries, while Annsleigh Anderson was efficient through the air with 97 yards and a score on 72.2% completion. Miriah Lopez caught 6 passes for 80 yards and a touchdown. The Trojans remain scoreless through three games, with their quarterbacks combining for just 59 passing yards on the day.

Kansas City, MO
Kansas City Glory23
Oregon Ravens14

Maria Fautali put on another clinic, needing just 6 carries to rack up 111 rushing yards and 2 touchdowns — including a 71-yard scoring run — as the Glory improved to 2-0 at home. The Ravens drew first blood, but Kansas City answered in electric fashion — Kassidy Snowden took the ensuing kickoff 85 yards for a touchdown that immediately swung momentum back to Kansas City. Oregon got a solid performance from Whitney Gifford, who rushed for 67 yards and 2 touchdowns, but the Ravens couldn't keep up with Kansas City's firepower. The Glory improve to 2-1 while Oregon falls to 1-2.

Kent, WA
Golden State Storm23
Seattle Majestics18

Seattle came out of the gate hot — Adriana Gutierrez took the opening kickoff 85 yards for a touchdown to set the tone — but the expansion Storm answered and picked up their first road win in team history, improving to 2-1 behind Kris Grimes' team-leading 127 rushing yards on 23 carries. Stephanie Elizondo added 54 passing yards and a touchdown while Sylvia Sloss contributed 20 rushing yards and a score plus 44 receiving yards. Alaina Valmassei threw for 77 yards and a touchdown and Kay Lewis rushed for 56 yards, but the Majestics fall to 0-3 on the season. Leilani Caamal continues to anchor the Storm defense with a league-leading 46 combined tackles.

Las Vegas, NV
San Diego Rebellion24
Las Vegas Silver Stars6

The Rebellion stayed perfect at 3-0, cruising past Las Vegas behind Danny Trainor's 110 passing yards and 2 touchdowns. Kesz Wesley added 84 rushing yards on the ground, while Taranisha Taylor hauled in 4 catches for 65 yards and a touchdown. Alicia Zappia-Neeley added a 45-yard receiving touchdown, and Brittani Lusain took a punt 80 yards to the house for a score. Jocelyn Charette was perfect on special teams with a WNFC-record 44-yard field goal and 3 PATs. Thaycha Rodriguez showed improvement for Las Vegas with 83 passing yards and a touchdown to Isabel Warren, but the Silver Stars remain winless at 0-3.

GAME OF THE WEEK
Texas Elite Spartans
2-0
vs
7:00 PM CST • Apr 18
Chicago Winds
1-1

The undefeated Texas Elite welcome the Chicago Winds to Arlington in the premier matchup of Week 4. Texas has been dominant through two games, outscoring opponents 40-12 behind Tara Thomas's 261 rushing yards and a defense led by Whitney Palmer's league-leading 5 sacks. Chicago comes in off a bye but will be looking to shake off the memory of their Week 2 shutout loss to Mississippi. Sarah Teubner's arm (220 yards, 4 TD on the season) will be tested against the Spartans' stingy defense, which has allowed the fewest points per game in the WNFC at just 6.0. This is a measuring-stick game for both teams with playoff implications already taking shape.

Atlanta Truth

Atlanta heads to Jacksonville needing a win to keep their season from slipping away at 1-2. The Truth are coming off a 25-15 loss to Mississippi in which they blew a 15-0 lead, and Renee Langlais will need to recapture her Week 2 form (218 yards, 3 TD) against a Florida defense that has allowed just 13 points in two games — tied for third-fewest in the league. The Avengers boast the league's top rushing attack at 209.5 yards per game, led by Mykayla Maddox (205 yards, 2 TD). This is a must-win for Atlanta in a competitive Atlantic Division.

Two Atlantic Division teams sitting at 1-1 meet in Paterson in a game with early divisional implications. The Wave and Prodigy have both been idle since Week 2, making this a pivotal moment for each team to establish momentum heading into the second half of the schedule. Washington's Ashley Clark (232 passing yards) will look to exploit a Wave defense that allowed 50 points to Atlanta in Week 2, while Jersey Shore's Karen Mulligan has 264 passing yards on the season despite having zero touchdown passes.

The undefeated Falconz host a Las Vegas team still searching for its first win of 2026. Utah has been quietly efficient through two games, outscoring opponents 32-22 behind Gina Mondragon's 143 rushing yards and a defense that allows just 11 points per game. The Silver Stars showed some offensive life in Week 3 with Thaycha Rodriguez throwing for 83 yards and a touchdown — her best game of the season — and Isabel Warren hauling in 2 interceptions on the defensive side. But Las Vegas has been outscored 92-15 on the season, making this a steep climb against a well-balanced Utah squad.
